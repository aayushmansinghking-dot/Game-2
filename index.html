Introduction

Rani Lakshmibai of Jhansi occupies a sacred place in Indian history. She was not only a queen but also a fearless warrior, visionary leader, and symbol of resistance against colonial injustice. At a time when women were denied public roles, she led armies, defended her kingdom, and sacrificed her life for freedom.

Family and Early Life

Born as Manikarnika Tambe on 19 November 1828 in Varanasi, she was raised by her father Moropant Tambe, a court official of Peshwa Baji Rao II. After her mother Bhagirathi Sapre‚Äôs early death, her father ensured she received a unique education combining academics and warfare.

Marriage, Son and Doctrine of Lapse

She married Maharaja Gangadhar Rao of Jhansi in 1842. After the death of their biological child, the couple adopted Damodar Rao. The British refusal to recognize him under the Doctrine of Lapse became the main cause of the conflict.
